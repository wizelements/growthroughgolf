Golf Lesson Plans

Ages 4–6 — Swing Basics Through Play
  • Beginner: Playful exploration with animal walks, foam sticks for grip and swing familiarity, and basic hand-eye coordination activities.
  • Intermediate: Introduces stance fundamentals, chipping basics with color-coded targets, and playful putting challenges.
  • Advanced: Drills to reinforce consistent swing rhythm, basic alignment skills, and peer-supported tempo activities.
Download PDF
Ages 7–9 — Posture and Precision
  • Beginner: Visual alignment games, grip correction drills, and posture-building activities using mirrors and guided feedback.
  • Intermediate: Swing mechanics focus, accuracy-based putting drills, and introductory chipping challenges with distance control.
  • Advanced: Enhanced ball-striking consistency, target selection strategies, and structured practice loops with corrections.
Download PDF
Ages 10–12 — Form, Focus & Flow
  • Beginner: Introduction to swing plane concepts, alignment games, and full swing practice using visual and tactile cues.
  • Intermediate: Development of distance management skills, precise cone-based target practice, and integrated posture drills.
  • Advanced: Pressure-focused game situations, swing analysis via video feedback, and implementation of mental strategies for on-course play.
Download PDF
Teens (13–17) — Power and Mental Game
  • Beginner: Swing refinement drills, impact position awareness, and developing foundational consistency through controlled practice.
  • Intermediate: Shot strategy and selection, course management scenarios, and routines for tempo and pacing.
  • Advanced: Advanced mental strategies including pressure simulations, journaling sessions for self-assessment, and competitive skill-building activities.
Download PDF
Adults — Functional Golf for Everyday Players
  • Beginner: Comfortable setup exercises, posture resets, slow-motion swing drills for building consistent contact.
  • Intermediate: Iron-play control drills, tempo-focused swing improvements, and situational scenario practices (sand, rough, slopes).
  • Advanced: Performance tracking, specialty shot training (fade, draw), and customized goal-oriented practice routines.
Download PDF
Seniors — Smooth Swings & Longevity
  • Beginner: Gentle mobility and posture exercises, chair-assisted swing drills, and comfort-focused grip practices.
  • Intermediate: Tempo refinement, swing fluidity exercises, visual and tactile alignment aids for improved consistency.
  • Advanced: Advanced distance control drills, guided visualization for swing consistency, and strategic play for reduced fatigue.

Gymnastics Lesson Plans

Walking–Age 3 (Tots) — Early Gym Play
  • Beginner: Spatial awareness, balance activities, gentle exploration with mats and obstacle courses.
  • Intermediate: Basic tumbling (forward rolls, log rolls), climbing, and assisted jumps.
  • Advanced: Tempo variations, social interaction games, independent circuit exploration.
Download PDF
Ages 4–6 (Preschool) — Gymnastics Foundations
  • Beginner: Fundamental shapes (tuck, straddle, pike), balancing on beams, jumping basics.
  • Intermediate: Skill progressions (cartwheels, rolls), dynamic jumping sequences, beginner beam routines.
  • Advanced: Linking movements into simple routines, improving strength and balance, rhythmic and coordinated sequences.
Download PDF
Ages 7–9 — Strength and Skill
  • Beginner: Sharpening motor skills, introducing handstands, controlled jumps and landings.
  • Intermediate: Posture improvement, strength conditioning (core, upper body), refining tumbling techniques (round-offs, bridges).
  • Advanced: Routine creation, power elements (front and back walkovers), detailed skill mastery, flexibility enhancement.
Download PDF
Ages 10–12 — Form & Flow
  • Beginner: Reinforcing gymnastics shapes, rhythm training, refining jumps and leaps.
  • Intermediate: Sequencing basic skills into routines, consistent tumbling skills (handsprings, advanced rolls), introducing rhythmic flow.
  • Advanced: Emphasizing control in transitions, advanced tumbling (aerials, complex combinations), choreographed floor routines.

Teens (13–17) — Advanced Gymnastics Skills
  • Beginner: Refinement of basic and intermediate skills, building gymnastics vocabulary, strength training foundations.
  • Intermediate: Skill specialization (bars, beam, vault), strength and conditioning enhancement, performance confidence building.
  • Advanced: High-level skill acquisition (back handsprings, layouts), personalized competitive preparation, advanced performance choreography.

Adults — Functional Gymnastics & Fitness
  • Beginner: Gentle introductory routines, improving mobility, posture, basic gymnastics conditioning.
  • Intermediate: Developing gymnastics strength, improving flexibility, introductory skill practice (rolls, handstands).
  • Advanced: Advanced gymnastics-based fitness, mastering inversion skills (handstands, cartwheels), bodyweight mastery routines.
